Thesis

I bought a stock that wiped out its own shareholders nine months ago. Wolfspeed filed Chapter 11 in June 2025, emerged in September, and the old equity got about 0.008 of a new share per share, less than one percent of the new company. What came out the other side kept the factories, the patents, and the single best position in one material, and shed roughly $4.6 billion of debt, 70 percent of the load, with the rest pushed to 2030. The franchise is world-class. The old equity was the casualty. I am buying the franchise on the new equity, after the reset.

The thesis is one line: the AI datacenter is moving to 800 volts of direct current, 800 volts runs on silicon carbide, and Wolfspeed is the purest, most upstream silicon carbide company on earth.

The physics forces it. A megawatt rack at 54 volts needs about 18,500 amps; at 800 volts it needs 1,250. Almost 15 times less current, and since resistive heating rises with the square of current, roughly 219 times less heat lost in the copper. NVIDIA's own numbers for the 800V switch: 45 percent less copper, up to 200kg of copper busbar per 1MW rack avoided, ~5 percent end-to-end efficiency gain, 30 percent lower TCO. The target is the Kyber rack with 576 Rubin Ultra GPUs, full production 2027, and NVIDIA named the entire power industry as partners. The standard is set.

Something has to switch that voltage, and plain silicon hits a wall there. Silicon carbide's bandgap is 3.26 eV versus silicon's 1.12, roughly ten times the breakdown field. It blocks the same voltage in a layer ten times thinner, switches without the tail current silicon IGBTs drag, and tolerates heat. Wolfspeed put a 10,000-volt SiC die into commercial use in March 2026. And we already ran this play: the Porsche Taycan went 800V in October 2019, Hyundai and Kia followed, and SiC inverters added more than 5 percent of range from efficiency alone. Same physics, same answer, two giant industries. That is gravity, not a fad.

Why Wolfspeed and not just "buy silicon carbide": it makes the material the chips are made of, with roughly a third of the global substrate market, and sells wafers to its own competitors. Infineon, ST, onsemi, and ROHM all buy from it. It is the only major pure-play, vertically integrated from crystal to device, built the world's first 200mm SiC fab at Mohawk Valley, and is shutting the last 150mm Durham line in 2026 to go all-in on 200mm. AI-datacenter revenue has doubled over the past three quarters. The demand vector is turning on right as everyone is still scared off by the bankruptcy.

Sized for asymmetry, not safety. Beta near 8, $8 to $80 in a year. I can watch it swing 50 percent in a week without flinching because I can explain it down to the breakdown field of the crystal.

  • Rack power path: ~120kW today, 600kW next generation, 1MW on the 2027 roadmap. Kyber rack: 576 Rubin Ultra GPUs, full 800V production 2027.
  • SiC material: 3.26 eV bandgap vs 1.12 for silicon, ~10x breakdown field. Device ratings 650/1200/1700V, 3.3-10kV in development.
  • Market: SiC device market ~$10B by 2030 at ~20% CAGR, but overcapacity and price war through 2027-2028. China ~40% of wafer capacity. Autos ~70% of SiC demand.

What Kills It

  • sic-overcapacity-price-war: the SiC market is in a real downturn with overcapacity and a price war that forecasts say does not clear until 2027 or 2028.
  • ev-demand-air-pocket: roughly 70 percent of SiC demand is still EVs, the exact market that already bankrupted this company once.
  • china-capacity-flood: China holds about 40 percent of global wafer capacity and can keep prices pinned.
  • datacenter-leg-slips-past-2027: the 800V ramp is a 2027 production story; if it slips, the new leg does not replace the EV hole in time.
  • balance-sheet-relapse: the bankruptcy is proof this franchise can be nearly vaporized; a repeat of heavy burn into a soft market goes to zero again.
