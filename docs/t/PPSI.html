Thesis

The purest "AI ran out of power and the grid can't keep up" bet I found, and it is a $45M nothing of a company. PRYMUS is on-site power you can drop in within months instead of waiting years for the grid: pre-engineered energy blocks from 1MW up to 10MW, mobile prime generation plus battery storage, aimed at modular and containerized data centers and AI compute. Site delivery to full power in about 6 months, against a 2 to 3 year grid upgrade wait. That gap is the entire product.

It just landed a real $6M order from a big national logistics customer, two 1.2MW systems, and the deliveries hit this year, not 2027 like people assumed. PowerCore is a second product with shipments starting H2 2026. The margin-collapse story everybody repeats is stale too. FY2025 gross margin did roughly halve during the new-product ramp, but it already bounced back in Q1 2026 and backlog turned up sequentially. The bear case was real and backward-looking. The bull case is a launched product with a hard-dollar booked order behind it. Tiny and early, but real. Sized like the lottery ticket it is.

What Kills It

1. Cash burn and dilution. Cash went from $41.6M to $15M and the company is loss-making. If PRYMUS revenue is slow, the next capital comes from printing shares at a $45M cap. 2. PRYMUS orders stall. The entire bull case is a promise that has produced exactly one $6M proof-point order so far. No second order by the volume-shipment window means the story was one customer, not a market. 3. Margin relapse. The Q1 bounce needs to hold as new systems ship. Another quarter near the FY2025 lows kills the "stale bear case" argument. 4. Single-order concentration. One logistics customer is the receipt. If that delivery slips or sours, there is nothing behind it yet. 5. Lumpy microcap revenue. $4.3M quarters that swing 37% mean any single print can gap this thing hard in either direction. Size accordingly.
