CRDO — Credo Technology

Machine-drafted 2026-07-01 from my own drafts and research notes. I review everything that carries my name.

Thesis

Everyone fights over the GPU. Credo makes the wires between them. Active electrical cables and the SerDes silicon that move data inside an AI cluster, the problem of keeping tens of thousands of chips fed fast enough to matter. FY26 revenue came in at $1.335 billion, roughly 3x, with Q4 up 157%. Gross margin 68%. Net income $662 million, up more than 5x. And management guides more than 80% growth for FY27. Already profitable, growing fast, the quiet interconnect tollbooth of the buildout.

The detail that killed the old bear case: customer concentration is improving, from 67% down to 34%. Five hyperscale AEC customers now. This stopped being a one-customer story a while ago.

The structural hedge is the part I like most. AECs are quietly replacing optics for short-reach hyperscaler links, and Credo owns that lane. But Credo also sells optical, and it bought DustPhotonics for ~$750 million to deepen that leg. So it collects on both halves of the wire. If copper holds the short-reach socket, Credo wins. If the optical transition comes faster, Credo still wins. The 200G/lane transition to 1.6T is underway and Credo is in it.

This is my networking anchor at 7.9% of book, average $209, built by adding on red days. On June 11 it was red while my account was up 5%, so I sold a green position to buy more of it. On June 30 it ripped on the interconnect story with the rest of the Physical Layer chain. Networking is my biggest layer at 24% of book, and CRDO is the biggest piece of it. The one I most want to add to if the tape gives me a pullback.

Connectivity is where the AI data center quietly bottlenecks. Credo sells the picks.

What Kills It

  • hyperscaler-concentration: concentration improved from 67% to 34% but a handful of hyperscalers still drive the revenue, and one lost program or in-source decision dents the ramp.
  • optics-leapfrogs-aec: if short-reach links jump straight to optics faster than expected, the copper AEC franchise shrinks and the DustPhotonics hedge has to carry more than it was priced for.
  • growth-guide-miss: a >80% FY27 guide is the multiple's whole spine; one guide-down quarter reprices the stock hard.
  • ai-capex-pause: interconnect demand is a direct derivative of cluster buildouts; if hyperscaler capex pauses, the wires get cut first.
  • interconnect-competition: the interconnect socket is contested; a competitor winning the 200G/lane generation at a major customer breaks the tollbooth framing.
