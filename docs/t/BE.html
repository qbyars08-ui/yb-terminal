Thesis

Bloom Energy makes solid oxide fuel cells (SOFCs) for behind-the-meter datacenter power. The pivot moment was the Oracle Project Jupiter announcement (April 27, 2026) — Oracle and BorderPlex publicly replaced previously planned gas turbines with Bloom fuel cells for a 1,400-acre 2.8 GW hyperscale AI campus in New Mexico.

That's the moment Bloom transitioned from "premium clean power" to "default behind-the-meter answer for AI datacenters."

The next decade of AI buildout is power-constrained. Grid interconnection queues are 2-3 years in major US datacenter markets. Behind-the-meter capacity is the only thing that scales fast enough. There are two paths:

  • Existing nuclear (VST, the Power layer anchor) — 20-year contracts with Microsoft + Meta
  • Fuel cells (BE, the Power layer pivot) — Oracle Project Jupiter validates

The bet is that two paths for behind-the-meter datacenter power are better than one.
