Thesis

HBM (high-bandwidth memory) is the AI bottleneck. The compute side has multiple paths (TSMC at 3nm/2nm, custom silicon at AVGO, GPUs at NVDA/AMD). The memory side is structurally constrained:

  • Three global HBM suppliers: SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron. That's it.
  • Sold out through calendar 2026 at every supplier on non-cancellable contracts. FY2027 already pricing forward.
  • Micron is the only US-domiciled supplier. CHIPS Act funding, supply-chain redundancy, US-customer preference under the current tariff posture all compound.
  • HBM3E 36GB shipping in volume to multiple hyperscaler customers. Tested first by NVIDIA on Blackwell-class accelerators.
  • HBM4 at 11 Gbps is industry-leading at sample stage, qualified with six partners for production ramp through calendar 2026 to 2027.

The book pays $354/sh for ownership of an unreplaceable bottleneck. Memory CAPEX cycles are ugly historically, but this cycle is structurally different: AI demand growth at 40% CAGR meets advanced-packaging supply growth in the high teens. The gap is pricing power.

Kill vectors (Predator checks)

1. HBM oversupply — if all three suppliers ramp simultaneously and demand growth slows, pricing collapses. Track: contract pricing in Q-on-Q reports, hyperscaler capex guidance. 2. Customer concentration loss — Micron's HBM revenue is concentrated in 2-3 hyperscaler customers. If NVDA, Microsoft, or Google qualifies a competitor's HBM3E and shifts allocation, revenue trajectory breaks. 3. Samsung qualification breakthrough — Samsung has lagged Hynix and Micron on HBM3 yields. If Samsung qualifies for major hyperscaler programs in 2026, the duopoly tightens and pricing power weakens for Micron. 4. Hynix cost edge — SK Hynix has been the leader on HBM yield. If their cost-per-die drops materially below Micron's, share shifts. 5. CHIPS Act / policy — a future administration cuts CHIPS Act funding or imposes tariffs that reshape memory economics. Less likely than supply/demand vectors but not zero.

Why this name vs peers

  • vs SK Hynix (000660.KS) — Hynix is the global leader on HBM share. ADR-only access, listed in Korea, US-investor liquidity is thinner. Micron is the US-domiciled play with similar exposure and better trading liquidity.
  • vs Samsung (005930.KS) — Samsung is the laggard on HBM3 yields. Wider business than just memory (logic, mobile, displays) so memory upside is diluted in the share price. Micron is the pure-play memory bet.
  • vs WDC / STX (storage) — completely different bottleneck. Storage is HDD/SSD; HBM is DRAM-class. Not substitutable.
  • vs LRCX / AMAT / KLAC (memory equipment) — picks-and-shovels of memory, but they sell to all three memory companies. They benefit from the cycle as a whole; Micron benefits from share-of-cycle. Higher beta to Micron's specific execution.
