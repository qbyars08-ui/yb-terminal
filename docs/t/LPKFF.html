Thesis

LPKF is a German maker of laser-based manufacturing systems. The headline number looks ugly because the legacy segments are ugly. PCB demand is soft on the consumer electronics cycle and the Chinese solar build-out that fed the etching segment is done. That is not why I own it.

I own it for LIDE, laser-induced deep etching of glass. Advanced AI chip packaging is moving from organic substrates to glass, and Intel, Samsung, and TSMC are all pushing that inflection. LPKF's Vitrion S 5000 is the laser system built for exactly that work, and Q1 brought the first LIDE capacity expansion order. So the bet is simple: the dying segments mark the price, the glass-substrate wave marks the future, and the order book already leans the right way. It is early, it is small, and it is still proving the thesis. I added on a down 6% day on 2026-05-27 because the friction of a foreign-listed name is part of why it stays cheap.

What Kills It

1. Glass substrate adoption slips. If Intel, Samsung, and TSMC push glass packaging out a generation, the LIDE order flow dries up and this is just a shrinking industrial laser company. 2. Solar drag persists. Chinese solar capacity is fully built. If that segment stays structurally compressed, it keeps eating the P&L while LIDE ramps. 3. PCB cycle stays weak. The consumer electronics recovery that is supposed to floor the legacy business may not show in 2026. 4. Rotation. Sized small on purpose. If a higher-conviction Photonics seat opens, this is a natural funding candidate. Down 4 straight quarters with no improving thesis means rotate.
