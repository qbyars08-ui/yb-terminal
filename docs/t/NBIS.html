Thesis

Nebius is a neutral-cloud GPU infrastructure provider with two customer backlogs that anchor the next 4-6 quarters of revenue:

  • $19.4B Microsoft contract — long-dated GPU capacity commitment.
  • $27B Meta contract — even larger long-dated commitment, signed Q1'26.
  • Combined ~$46B forward revenue. That's a multiple of the company's market cap, locked under contract.

Two operational milestones drive the thesis:

  • Finland datacenter live — first major buildout, supplying capacity now.
  • Iceland datacenter next — geothermal power + Nordic cool air = best-in-class PUE economics. Coming online to absorb the Microsoft/Meta backlog scale-up.

The bet is that Nebius is the cleanest neutral-cloud play: not vertically integrated like AWS/Azure/GCP (which compete for the same customers), not power-constrained like behind-the-meter utility deals, not narrative-only like some of the AI compute SPACs. Real backlog, real datacenters, real customers, growing at 547% YoY at last print.

Why anchor

NBIS passes the manifesto signature-line test:

"I buy the exact raw materials, hardware machines, and power plants that force artificial intelligence to actually exist and operate in the real world."

Datacenter capacity IS the hardware machine. The GPU clusters running every AI workload have to live somewhere with power, cooling, networking, and floor space. Nebius is one of the few public-equity ways to own that infrastructure layer directly — most of it is private (Crusoe, Lambda) or buried inside hyperscaler P&Ls (Azure, AWS).

Kill vectors (Predator checks)

1. Microsoft contract cancellation — $19.4B is the floor. If Microsoft moves capacity to Azure-internal or to a competitor (CoreWeave, Crusoe), the thesis breaks immediately. 2. Meta contract cancellation — $27B. Same dynamic. Meta is more likely than Microsoft to bring capacity in-house long-term, so this vector is structurally larger. 3. Finland or Iceland execution slip — datacenter buildouts are full of construction, permitting, power-delivery, and equipment-supply risk. A 2-quarter slip on Iceland with the Microsoft/Meta capacity ramp pacing against it changes the revenue trajectory materially. 4. Neoclouds margin compression — if CoreWeave, Lambda, and Crusoe scale faster on more cost-effective power, NBIS's cost-per-GPU-hour rises relative to peers and customers exit at contract end. 5. Hyperscaler in-house build — the strategic risk: Microsoft, Meta, Amazon all have capacity to build their own neutral-cloud-style infrastructure. NBIS thesis assumes they don't because of focus / opportunity cost. If they pivot, the customer base evaporates over 4-8 quarters.
