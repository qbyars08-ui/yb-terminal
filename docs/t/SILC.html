SILC — Silicom

Machine-drafted 2026-07-01 from my own drafts and research notes. I review everything that carries my name.

Thesis

Silicom is about 4 percent of my book, and it is the cheapest asymmetry in it. A small Israeli company that builds the networking layer inside other companies' hardware. FPGA smart NICs, edge appliances, white-label switches. Design wins. Once you are engineered into a customer's product, ripping you out means re-qualifying the whole platform. Boring, sticky, real.

The setup is the hole. $150.6M revenue and $2.73 EPS at the 2022 peak, then the telco and SD-WAN customers choked on their own double-ordered inventory and revenue collapsed 61 percent to a $58.1M trough in 2024. The market called it a melting ice cube and walked. It missed the flip. Q1 2026 came in at $19.1M, up 33 percent, core guided to roughly 40 percent growth in Q2, full-year guide $82-83M. Five straight quarters of sequential growth. The design-win engine is now pointed at AI inference, post-quantum crypto upgrades, and white-label switching at the same time.

The lottery ticket is the part almost nobody has modeled. On June 30 Silicom announced its first AI-inference production order. A May proof of concept with an unnamed AI-compute challenger aimed at Tier-1 hyperscalers converted into a real order. The product is a field-updatable FPGA card, not a frozen ASIC, and management frames inference as a networking problem, which is the exact thing Silicom has done for thirty years. If the full PoC succeeds, the customer anticipates tens of thousands of units at multi-thousand-dollar prices. That is a $90-150M revenue event, bigger than all of Silicom today. Management's honest timing: significant inference revenue is probably 2027.

Then the engine that makes a micro-cap violent. Only ~5.7M shares out, float ~5.4M, because the company bought back roughly 23 percent of itself in the down years. $63-76M net cash, zero debt, a quarter or more of the market cap. Breakeven sits around $90-100M of annual revenue, and above that line every extra $10M of revenue adds roughly 55 cents of EPS. Management's own target model is more than $3 in EPS on $150-160M of revenue. That is a swing from a $2 loss to a $3 profit on a float this thin.

The honest read: the base case (~$35-48 by 2028) brackets the current price, so the recovery is mostly priced. $100 is the bull case and it needs the inference deployment to land at scale on top of a recovered $90-110M core. I own it as inference optionality on an inflecting core, sized for a violent, illiquid round trip. Sub-$43 is the add zone, high $30s is better, under $30 is bear-case fair value and a back-up-the-truck level if the thesis holds.

What Kills It

  • poc-fails-to-convert: the whole crazy case hangs on an unnamed customer's proof of concept that does not even finish until H2 2026, and a PoC can simply die.
  • customer-concentration-drawdown: top three customers are ~28% of revenue and a single drawdown is the exact failure mode that already blew this company up once.
  • recovery-stall-multiple-compression: stall the core and the multiple compresses back to the high teens or low $20s, a 50-70 percent drawdown with the 2023-24 meltdown as the template.
  • micro-float-violence: a 5.4M-share float means a bad print can take it down 20 percent in a session.
  • israel-geopolitical-exposure: an Israeli operation with geopolitical and currency baggage, and mostly North American, tariff-exposed revenue.
