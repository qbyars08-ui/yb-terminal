PENG — Penguin Solutions

Machine-drafted 2026-07-01 from my own drafts and research notes. I review everything that carries my name.

Thesis

My two largest anchors are memory (MU) and neocloud compute (NBIS). Penguin is the rare company that is both. It started in 1988 as a specialty memory company and is now what it calls the AI Factory Platform Company. It designs, builds, and runs full AI data center infrastructure, and it sells the integrated memory that goes inside it. Memory plus the AI factory, in one ticker. Micron and Nebius, holding hands.

The customer list is the exact tell I look for. Penguin sells into enterprises, into neocloud providers, and into sovereign AI, the national governments now racing to build their own capacity. Every country that decides it needs its own AI infrastructure is a customer for a company that builds AI factories for a living. And the piece I like most: Penguin is leaning into inference with its OriginAI platform and a new memory server built to make inference cheaper. Training built the first wave. Inference is the bigger, longer one, and that is where Penguin is aiming. I own it, I love it, and I added to it again into the June MU print.

The Numbers That Matter

  • FY26 growth guide raised in June to roughly 12%, up from 6%
  • Revenue guided to about $1.6B vs Street near $1.5B, stock jumped more than 20% on the raise
  • Roughly $2.8B company doing about $1.4B in trailing revenue
  • Q2 FY26 revenue $343M, five new AI/HPC customers added, including neoclouds and sovereign-government entities
  • Position: 2.8% of book, +34% (2026-06-28 read)

What Kills It

1. Guide raise fizzles. The 12% number is the whole re-rate. If the next print walks it back, the 20% pop unwinds. 2. Sovereign orders are lumpy. Government AI-factory deals are big, slow, and political. A pushed contract can blow a quarter. 3. Hyperscaler crowd-out. Penguin's lane is the next 50 data centers that are not hyperscalers. If hyperscalers eat the neocloud and sovereign niches, the lane narrows. 4. Memory cycle turn. The memory side of the business rides the same cycle as MU. Oversupply hits both halves of my thesis at once.
