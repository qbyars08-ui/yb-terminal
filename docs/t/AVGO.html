Thesis

AVGO is the only company that simultaneously owns two of the most defensible bottlenecks in AI infrastructure:

Layer 1 — Custom AI silicon (Compute IP):

  • Google TPU — Broadcom is the silicon design partner for Google's custom TPU chips. Multi-generation relationship. TPU is the most credible alternative to NVDA in production AI workloads.
  • Meta MTIA — Broadcom designs Meta's custom AI inference silicon. Meta is one of the largest AI infrastructure spenders.
  • Combined: Broadcom captures revenue and margin on every chip Google or Meta builds for their internal AI compute. That's the highest-quality bottleneck in the entire compute stack.

Layer 2 — Ethernet switch silicon (Networking):

  • ~90% market share in datacenter Ethernet switch ASIC silicon. Dominant for years.
  • Tomahawk 5 and successors are the de facto standard for hyperscaler datacenter networking.
  • AI workloads need Ethernet — InfiniBand (Mellanox / NVDA) is the alternative, but Ethernet's openness, multi-vendor support, and ecosystem economics keep it dominant in non-NVDA shops.

The dual-layer position is the moat. Even if one layer compresses, the other carries the thesis. Both layers are sticky multi-year customer relationships, not transactional commodity sales.

Why anchor

AVGO passes the manifesto signature-line test:

"I buy the exact raw materials, hardware machines, and power plants that force artificial intelligence to actually exist and operate in the real world."

Custom AI silicon IS the hardware that forces AI to run. Ethernet switch silicon IS the hardware that connects the GPU clusters. Both are unreplaceable substrate-class infrastructure. Without AVGO, Google TPU and Meta MTIA don't exist; without AVGO, hyperscaler Ethernet networking has no alternative supplier at scale.

Kill vectors (Predator checks)

1. Google TPU loss — if Google switches custom silicon partner (unlikely given the multi-generation relationship and TPU IP integration), the highest-margin AI silicon revenue stream evaporates. 2. Meta MTIA loss — if Meta moves MTIA design in-house or to a different partner, the second-largest custom AI silicon revenue stream evaporates. 3. Ethernet share erosion — Marvell and others have been gaining in some segments. If Tomahawk 5 successors miss their schedule or yield, share could drop materially. 4. VMware execution — VMware acquisition added software exposure. If integration goes badly or the SaaS-pivot underperforms, the infrastructure-software part of the AVGO P&L drags. 5. Customer concentration — Google and Meta are massive customers. If either reduces capex meaningfully (recession, AI buildout pause), AVGO's growth trajectory compresses.
