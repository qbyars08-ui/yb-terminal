Thesis

Training a model is the expensive thing you do occasionally. Running it is the thing you do a billion times a day. That is inference, and inference does not want to live in one hyperscale building in Virginia. It wants to live close to where the data is born: the telco network, the factory floor, the drone, the battlefield. The reasons are boring and that is exactly why it happens: latency, cost, power, and data you are not legally allowed to ship to a cloud. If inference moves to the edge, someone sells the gear. OSS sells the rugged box.

One Stop Systems builds ruggedized high-performance computers for the edge. GPU servers, PCIe expansion units, solid-state storage arrays engineered to run AI where a normal datacenter rack would die: heat, shock, vibration, a moving vehicle, a ship, a battlefield. Their tagline says it: built for the edge, engineered for the fight. Customers are defense contractors, government, autonomy programs, industrial. This is who you call when you need Nvidia-class compute to survive somewhere a data hall never could.

The numbers are inflecting. Q1 revenue grew 55% to $8.1 million, gross margin jumped to 51.6%, book-to-bill hit 1.8x, meaning orders come in almost twice as fast as they ship. Three straight quarters of positive adjusted EPS, zero debt, roughly $56 million net cash against a $434 million market cap, and a pipeline north of $1 billion. In June they landed an $8.4 million initial defense contract, 91 rugged 3U servers for low-band signal surveillance, with stated potential around $44 million over four years. In April they signed to supply rugged AI compute for a network of autonomous energy nodes.

The variant perception: the market still files OSS under sleepy hardware box maker. What changed is the company. New CEO Michael Knowles is a defense-industry operator. They divested Bressner, the low-margin European distribution arm, to become a pure-play rugged edge-AI compute business. That is why gross margin went from the low 40s to the low 50s. Book-to-bill near 1.8x plus Russell 2000 inclusion plus a defense order book that recurs is the early innings of a defense-AI razor-and-blade, not a box maker.

The honest part: you are paying up. At about $17.50 the stock trades near 12 times sales, it just 5x'd off its lows, the three covering analysts sit around a $19 target, and 12% of the float is sold short. I entered around $17 and it sits at roughly 5 to 6% of my book, the higher-momentum, higher-multiple sibling to SILC in the same edge-inference trade. The business is genuinely inflecting, the defense angle is real, and the valuation already knows it. I size it for volatility and I am clear-eyed that the easy multiple expansion is behind it.

Own the layer the migration has to buy.

  • single-customer-half-of-revenue: one customer is ~51% of revenue, so a program loss or delay there is not a dent, it is a crater.
