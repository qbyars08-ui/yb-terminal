Thesis

Start with the leader so you have a measuring stick. IonQ did about $130 million in revenue in 2025, up 202%, and the market values it at more than $10 billion. North of 80 times sales. IQM, the company hiding inside RAAQ, did about $36 million in 2025 and is going public at roughly $1.8 billion pre-money. About 50 times sales. A real, revenue-generating, hardware-shipping European quantum champion at a lower multiple than the American name everyone crowds into. I own 200 shares at $11. I bought before the listing, not after the pop. Shareholders voted Thursday June 25 and RAAQ becomes IQMX, the first European quantum computing company on the Nasdaq.

The differentiator is the business model. IonQ, Rigetti, IBM, Google mostly rent you cloud access. IQM sells you the actual machine, installed in your building, owned by you. They have sold more on-premises quantum computers than anyone in the world. Think about who needs that: a national lab that cannot run sensitive workloads on someone else's cloud, a government buying quantum sovereignty, a supercomputing center that wants the quantum box bolted in next to the classical one. That is exactly who is buying. Machines in four of the world's top ten supercomputing centers. Leibniz runs four IQM systems. Julich, VTT, Cineca, the Taiwan Semiconductor Research Institute. And in June, Pathfinder, a 20-qubit Radiance, lit up at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, home of Frontier. When a lab buys the box instead of renting the cloud, that customer is not leaving.

The AI hook is why it earns a seat in this book at all. Quantum is not replacing the GPU. It is becoming a co-processor in the same data center, doing the math GPUs are bad at. NVIDIA's NVQLink launched supporting 17 quantum hardware builders and IQM is one of them, on the same list as IonQ, Rigetti, and Quantinuum. On June 22 HPE named IQM a quantum partner in its hybrid platform fusing quantum machines with HPE Cray supercomputers. IQM's machines already run as a scheduled resource inside a supercomputer, slotting in like just another node. The data center is the prize, and IQM builds the box that goes in it.

The receipts are real. More than 20 systems sold to roughly 13 customers. Around $465 million in cash at close to fund the roadmap. J.P. Morgan and Rothschild advised. Over €600 million raised privately before this, including a $320 million Series B last September at a $1 billion valuation, backed by BlackRock, the European Investment Bank, and Finnish and German government quantum programs. Seven-year-old hardware company, spun out of Aalto University and VTT in 2018, ~350 employees, run by co-founder Jan Goetz. Full-stack: chip, control electronics, cryogenics, software.

The roadmap: Radiance ships at 20 and 54 qubits today with a 150-qubit version due by end of 2026. Halocene starts at 150 qubits with a path to 1,000, two-qubit gate fidelity targeted at 99.7% climbing toward 99.95%, fault-tolerant quantum computing by 2030. And the European angle nobody prices: when IQM lists, it becomes the default way for an entire continent of capital to buy this theme. That buying does not exist at the SPAC price. It shows up after.

Quantum is the most speculative room in the Physical Layer house, and I keep my position there small by design. But inside that room, this is the seat I want.
