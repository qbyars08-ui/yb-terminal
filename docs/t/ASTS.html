Sleeve classification: Adjacent Tech

ASTS is held but explicitly NOT part of the Physical Layer of AI thesis. It lives on the live map under "Adjacent Tech." This is the honesty layer of the brand — a long-term tech bet that doesn't pass the manifesto signature-line test.

"I buy the exact raw materials, hardware machines, and power plants that force artificial intelligence to actually exist and operate in the real world."

ASTS makes satellites. Satellites are a service AI uses (connectivity for edge devices, data backhaul), not a substrate AI runs on. It's a real bet on orbital connectivity economics, but it's not an AI infrastructure tollbooth. That distinction matters and the sleeve makes it explicit.

Thesis

AST SpaceMobile is building a satellite constellation that delivers cellular connectivity directly to standard mobile phones — no special hardware, no terminal, just a normal phone connecting via a low-earth-orbit satellite when terrestrial cell towers aren't in range.

The bet is structural: most of the Earth's surface has no cell coverage. Today, that means service gaps. Tomorrow, with a working LEO direct-to-cell constellation, every mobile phone has connectivity everywhere. The TAM is essentially every mobile subscriber globally.

Two contractual anchors:

  • AT&T agreement — multi-year commercial partnership for AT&T-branded direct-to-cell service.
  • Verizon agreement — similar partnership.
  • Combined: two of the three largest US carriers committed to ASTS-delivered direct-to-cell service.

Defense + government use cases add a second customer column (military communications, emergency services, first responders).

Kill vectors (Predator checks)

1. Constellation deployment slip — ASTS revenue is gated on satellites in orbit. Launch delays, satellite manufacturing ramp issues, or operational failures push the revenue start. 2. Regulatory spectrum loss — direct-to-cell uses cellular spectrum. Regulatory frameworks for cellular-band satellite service vary by country; if a key market denies coverage, the international thesis compresses. 3. Starlink direct-to-cell competition — SpaceX's Starlink has launched direct-to-cell capability with T-Mobile. If Starlink's economics or coverage close ASTS's window before ASTS reaches scale, the carrier agreements re-negotiate at unfavorable terms. 4. Capital raise overhang — ASTS has historically required dilutive raises to fund constellation deployment. Continued raises pressure the share price even when execution is on track. 5. Customer concentration — heavy reliance on AT&T + Verizon. If either pulls back commitment depth, revenue trajectory compresses.
