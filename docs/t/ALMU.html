Thesis

Aeluma is the substrate underneath the next generation of optical interconnect. Indium phosphide quantum-dot photonic integrated circuits.

The bandwidth ceiling on AI clusters is becoming a photonic problem. Copper interconnect doesn't scale past a certain density; coherent optical solves the distance problem at the rack scale; quantum-dot photonic ICs are the architecture that scales further.

Aeluma is making the materials. They have:

  • 6 government R&D contracts (DARPA / DoD / DoE pipeline)
  • NASA award (April 21, 2026) specifically for quantum-dot laser commercialization
  • $4M+ new contracts in April alone
  • $38.6M cash = ~3 years of runway
  • Government has effectively underwritten Phase 1 commercialization at zero shareholder dilution

Pre-commercial photonic IC platform at venture pricing (~$340M market cap).

Why anchor

ALMU passes the manifesto signature-line test:

"I buy the exact raw materials, hardware machines, and power plants that force artificial intelligence to actually exist and operate in the real world."

Aeluma is the raw materials level — the InP wafer substrate that the photonic transceivers and photonic-quantum chips of the next decade get built on. Without InP at scale, the bandwidth ceiling stays where it is and AI cluster economics break.

Why this name vs peers

  • vs IONQ, RGTI, QBTS — those are quantum compute primaries (trapped-ion, superconducting, annealing). ALMU is the substrate layer underneath photonic-quantum, not a competing modality. Different bet entirely.
  • vs XNDU — XNDU is the only photonic-quantum compute pure-play (room-temperature qubits on optical fiber, GBS sampling). ALMU is the materials-and-laser-sources layer that XNDU and every other photonic-quantum company eventually buys from.
  • vs LITE / COHR — those are scaled-up optical component manufacturers. ALMU is the upstream IP + materials supplier. Smaller cap, earlier in the curve, more asymmetric upside if commercial product ships.
